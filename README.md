# Electromechanical-Tower-Actuation-System
## Some Background
Particularly in the Southeast, white-tailed deer pose a major problem for the farming industry. Deer enter fields and feed on crops, causing millions of dollars in agricultural damage each year. Although many efforts have been made to reduce this issue, they are often only partially effective. Some farmers obtain special permits to hunt the deer, while others spray deterrents on their crops or plant hedges of undesirable vegetation around their fields to discourage deer from entering.  
## The Solution by AU Agriculture
Working with local farmers, the university’s Department of Agriculture has proposed an autonomous solution to help reduce crop damage caused by deer. Traditional methods such as hunting, chemical deterrents, and decoy vegetation each have drawbacks, including wildlife loss, health concerns, labor, and cost. To address these limitations, the proposed system uses an AI-operated drone that will approach detected deer and scare them away using loud noises and bright flashes.

At this stage, the AI system is still being trained. As shown in the figure below, a tower has been erected with a thermal camera mounted at the top. The camera records video footage of deer activity and stores it on a hard drive. This hard drive is periodically collected, and the recorded footage is used to train the AI software that will eventually guide the drone’s autonomous operation.

This project can effectively be broken up into three subassemblies including the thrust collar, the travel nut, and the motor coupling. 
# The thrust collar

The thrust collar carries the axial load generated by the tower, preventing that load from being transferred directly into the motor mount. The figure below depicts the components of the assemlby.

<img width="663" height="494" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1e4e732a-9d6f-454f-9fd3-f22b0ba957c1" />
_Thrust Collar_

Callout 1 refers to the 5 TPI Acme stainless steel lead screw, callout 2 is its respective high strength hex nut, callout 3 shows a high strength hardened flat washer, callout 4 is a thrust bearing, and callout 5 is the thrust collar assembly pivot. The purpose of this assembly is to take axial load off of the motor-screw coupling, which will be detailed later. It is undesirable for the nuts to translate linearly on the screw, so they must be secured via permanent adhesive. The purpose of the washer is to evenly distribute pressure from the nut to the bearing and to prevent the galling thereof. The bearing is in place to allow for low friction rotation of the screw relative to the hex nut.
